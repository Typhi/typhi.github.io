Selfishness
Enhance: Playing with a friend enhances the gaming experience Strong

The game is more fun with a friend to experience the game with. It adds additional gameplay value in the form of a valuable partner or opponent to beat.

You can also have in-depth discussions about the game when you're not playing.

This added benefit can also be clear to the friend being shared with, which improves the strength of the conversion.

Trying to get someone to play World of Warcraft with you so that you can have fun together and discuss it outside of the game as well.

Age of Empires multiplayer

Diplomacy and inter-player interactions are an important aspect of the game, especially when the game itself isn't providing much action without it. Can be nice to prepare plans with your friend about how you'll conquer the world, for example.

There's a multi-player specific feature in the form of towns that can be founded and built up together with other players. A strong feature in theory, but the town ultimately becomes more and more meaningless after the early game.

Towns can also participate in Fort Battles, which are tactical real-time fights between rivaling towns for glory.

GvG has some potential for this, but:

  • Weakened by the fact that it's very difficult to get into
  • Weakened because you can't meaningfully participate until later in the game
  • Segmentation by era
  • A way to have a real-time battle with a friend to see who is better. Keep track of score
  • Show your friends' scores for campaign map battles
  • Show your friends' progress on:
    • Tech tree
    • Campaign map
  • Show your guildmate's progress on Guild Expeditions map
Get help: A friend's presence can actively and materially help you in the core gameplay Medium

You are often reminded of how a friend could improve your experience when playing the game in the core gameplay, a friend is important to your gaming experience and you can rationalize inviting a friend as being of benefit for both parties.

This added benefit can also be clear to the friend being shared with, which improves the strength of the conversion.

It's important that the fact that someone is a "real life" friend can differentiate them from random other players, otherwise it's easier to just get the help from the existing player base and you won't go to the effort of inviting someone.

Player awareness of this multiplayer aspect can be important to address - they might need reminders from time to time and while we as the game dev team are very aware of how important cooperation is, new players might not be.

Inviting someone to a farm game where everyone has to specialize in a certain area of the game so that you have another trading partner

A player can involve themselves in a challenge you're facing to help you overcome it.

Gifting is a central aspect of the game and gifts can regularly be given.

Other players can help attack and defend with their own armies, which is a central gameplay component. More friends means more survival. Very strongly present in the game concept.

  • Weakened by blocks in place against cooperation on the same internet connection.
  • Weakened by only being able to do this very well in the first days after starting - afterwards, your friend will have fallen far behind. (Planned: give a village to a friend so they can join at a later point in the game)
  • Was strengthened greatly by the addition of being able to invite someone to nearby your location, so that the invited player would become more relevant for the inviter.

Covered to a certain degree by towns (see above).

  • Weakened by it being hard to directly interact with other players beyond the town - you can't set up a private trade for example.

There's a real-time multiplayer tactical fight feature called Adventures, but it's weak because of how it's irrelevant for the core gameplay.

There is aiding, but it doesn't require a friend, just some random player to press a button. One person is the same as the next.

There are GB contributions, but it doesn't require a friend - just some random player to press a button. One person is the same as the next.

(Planned: Add some way for friends to meaningfully improve each other's city performance in the form of friend boosting.)
  • Have players choose their goods deposits so that you can specialize in certain goods, adding a more intentional social-cooperation aspect to trading. You'll see guilds organizing their goods deposits and players coaching their friends to go for certain goods.
  • Some way for a player to help out another one in PVE
  • Players you invite to the game provide a significantly stronger version of polivate to each other than a random player would.
  • Some location-based feature that gives an advantage to playing within roughly the same geographical location (unfortunately we're missing actual gameplay that can be done together).
Get incentive: You are given a reward by the game Weak / Medium (If it can be delivered at a time precisely when you need that item!)

Differentiated from the one above because it can easily be a fleeting 1-time experience of no lasting impact for your experience of the game. It can also add hesitation on your part to send an invitation because of your selfish reasons for doing so and also introduce hesitation on the part of the friend who is possibly aware of your motivations.

Getting diamonds in Forge for inviting someone

Various increasingly strong rewards: Premium currency, cool items, even a unique item.

Decoration when someone reaches iron age, diamonds at reaching later ages.
  • Change incentive to be something that can be of immediate use in certain circumstances. Prompt player when they are in those circumstances. Example: not enough diamonds? invite!

  • Survive something (treasure hunt expiry) with the action of a friend.

Brag: Show off something you accomplished Medium

It taps slightly into the same emotions as the one above, but there's a feeling of personal pride instead of general happiness. Only works if you can show off something of value for an external observer. Example: impressive city in SimCity versus 5k villages in Tribal Wars.

Gives us a chance to show off what kind of features the game has.

 "This farm I built in Farmville is pretty cool, check out what I did!" Missing Missing Missing
  • Show off your city on a social network
  • Celebrate finishing an event questline ("I finished the MLK story!" could be pretty cool to show off our nice events)
  • Share a fight replay
  • Celebrate entering a new era
  • Include a recent unit research when sharing the invite link on Facebook: "player name recently researched this cool unit + image of unit on battlefield" to add a personal touch to the otherwise generic advertisement
