How do they help?

  • They help us validate our hypothesis
  • They can teach us something for the future
  • Data to use that contradict the feedback
  • Safety net for unforeseen impacts

What do they cost us?

  • Implementation and testing costs/complexity
  • Attention resources

Important: A/B tests shouldn’t be used to design for us, and they shouldn’t be used as a way to avoid making a decision.

A/B Test Checklist

  • Have a compelling hypothesis
    • Include explanation about how behavior will (or won’t) change
  • Make the change bold: significantly change the experience in order to raise your chances of actually having an impact.
    • BOLD: new feature
    • BOLD: 100% change to a balancing number
    • NOT: Regenerate an energy resource every 45 minutes instead of every 60 minutes.
  • Try before you test: Give the changed situation a solid play to see if there are unforeseen elements to be aware of.
